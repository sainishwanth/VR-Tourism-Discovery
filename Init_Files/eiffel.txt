The Eiffel Tower, an iconic symbol of Paris and one of the most recognizable landmarks globally, stands as a testament to human engineering prowess and artistic innovation. Designed by Gustave Eiffel for the 1889 World's Fair, the tower initially faced criticism but soon captivated the world with its elegance and structural ingenuity. Rising to a height of 324 meters (1,063 feet), it held the title of the world's tallest man-made structure for over four decades. Today, the Eiffel Tower attracts millions of visitors annually, offering panoramic views of the City of Light and serving as an enduring symbol of France's rich cultural heritage and architectural excellence.
